Letter

Please join over 80 small business owners and sign our petition to city council.

Dear Seattle City Council,

As you know, our state has the country’s most regressive tax code, where poor people pay about seven times as much of their income in tax as wealthy people. Fortunately, Seattle is home to many successful companies that embrace creativity and innovation, presenting unique opportunity for alleviating the economic burdens faced by so many of our most vulnerable residents. Tapping into this potential is the spirit and intent of the Employee Hours Tax, which could generate $75M more in revenue for the city annually - 80% of which would go directly into securing affordable housing and 20% for human services. There is broad interest and support in Seattle’s diverse business community to help create a more equitable city through an EHT and other progressive solutions.
